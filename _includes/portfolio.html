Experience

Butterfield Engineering

For my first co-op in the summer of 2015, I worked with this Cincinnati-based contracting company. I feel this was a great experience because it allowed me to work on a variety of different projects at some of the largest companies in the Cincinnati area. One week I would be working on data collection and analysis systems and the next I could be doing anything from image processing and data visualization to helping administration build a more responsive website.

A few of the projects I was able to contribute to include:

  • Migrating an older codebase to a more maintainable, object-oriented approach.
  • Help develop version-control standards (mostly based on gitflow) and roll out Git to the entire company.
  • Model a data collection process and create a database to store testing results and metadata.
  • Automatically change the perspective of visual data using linear algebra and trig (this was before discovering the wonders of openCV unfortunately).
  • Design and build a basic relay board for a NI DAQ.

I was fortunate enough to be able to stay on part-time even after my co-op ended and I look forward to working full-time with them again during my next co-op semester this summer.

UC Robotics Team

Near the end of Fall semester I joined the UC Robotics Team and began working with them on the unmanned ground vehicle project. My primary contribution has been to the vision system. Working closely with Joe Hirschfeld and Doug Flick, we have automated the calibration of the cameras used on the self driving vehicle. We are currently working to transform a camera image to a laser scan, which is something akin to a 2D overhead map that tells the robot where obstacles are.
